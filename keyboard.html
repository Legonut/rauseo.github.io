Custom keyboard

In fall 2016, I became interested in building a custom keyboard. I desoldered the switches from another keyboard, and the PCB/Case/Caps were purchased. I wanted to create a plate that could support my non-standard layout and reduce noise. Since plastic is weaker than steel, I filled the empty spaces between the switches to increase bending strength. The plate was printed in two parts on my 3D printer. The result showed me that I still have much to learn when it comes to dimensioning 3D printed parts. The parts were good on the Y and Z axis, but the X axis shrunk, resulting in a gap between the two parts. Getting the size of the keywitch holes correct took only one prototype, but getting the sizing to be identical between all of the holes was difficult because the slicer didnt treat all of the holes the same.
